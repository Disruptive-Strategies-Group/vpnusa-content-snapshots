Our work

We fight extremism by working directly with people.

Violence Prevention Network USA takes a fundamentally different approach to preventing violent extremism. We don't preach to the choir — we engage directly with ideologically motivated individuals, helping them reject hate and violence.

The challenge we face

The scope of radicalization in the United States ranges from far-right extremism to Islamist ideology, from conspiracy movements to incel violence. These ideas spread just as easily on the dark web as they do in real-world settings.

Evidence suggests that violent extremism is outpacing available programming. The highly securitized, threat-focused nature of current US interventions has proven ineffective, and one-on-one specialist programs cannot scale fast enough.

We need a model that equips communities and influential figures in the lives of at-risk individuals — not just security specialists.

How we work

A community-centered model

Our work is built on a simple insight: the people best positioned to intervene are those who already have relationships and trust. Teachers, social workers, counselors, and faith leaders see radicalization early — and with the right tools, they can respond effectively.

Helpline & counseling

Confidential, anonymous, free-of-charge support for families, friends, and individuals concerned about radicalization.

RESTORE training

Evidence-based training program equipping community leaders with tools and methodologies for working with ideologically motivated individuals.

Direct engagement

Relationship-based work with individuals across the full spectrum of violent extremism, helping them embrace more democratic ways of thinking and acting.

Network building

Growing a professional network of prevention practitioners who share knowledge, support each other, and strengthen community capacity.

What guides us

Change is possible

We know from decades of experience and thousands of individual cases that people can and do leave extremist movements. Our work starts from this conviction.

Relationships over enforcement

Meaningful change comes through authentic human connection, not through threat and coercion. We engage without shaming, humiliation, or judgment.

Scale through communities

Specialist-only models cannot meet the scale of the problem. By training trusted community leaders, we multiply our impact across existing networks.

Evidence-based practice

Our methods are developed and continuously refined through direct practice with individuals across the ideological spectrum, informed by rigorous research.
