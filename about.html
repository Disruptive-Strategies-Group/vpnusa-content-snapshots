About us

We stop extremist violence before it happens.

Violence Prevention Network USA is a nonprofit working to stop extremist violence before it happens. We work directly with people at risk, the people around them, and the professionals who respond. Founded in Pittsburgh in 2022, we bring more than 25 years of practice in Germany and around the world.

 

Our story

From Berlin to Pittsburgh

Judy Korn founded Violence Prevention Network in Berlin on a straightforward conviction: if violence is learned, it can be unlearned.

As a teenager, she had watched neo-Nazi skinheads attack her friends. Instead of pulling away or writing the attackers off, she started a youth group and invited them to join. Engaging the people at the center of violence became the approach she built the organization around.

Since then, Violence Prevention Network has grown into one of the largest organizations of its kind in Europe. It has worked with thousands of people involved in violent extremism, built programs inside prisons, trained practitioners, and helped shape prevention and reintegration across Germany.

We brought the work to the United States and established Violence Prevention Network USA in Pittsburgh, an independent nonprofit set up to adapt these proven methods to American communities.

 

The Pittsburgh pilot

We chose Southwestern Pennsylvania to start.

Pittsburgh is a city living with the aftermath of the Tree of Life synagogue shooting. The region also has a strong network of local professionals and community leaders who are often the first to notice when something is shifting in a school, a family, a congregation, or a workplace.

In 2023 we launched the RESTORE Project, our first U.S. pilot. Over the pilot's three years, we trained more than 60 frontline professionals, grew our referral network to over 50 organizations, and developed a base of practitioners across education, behavioral health, reentry, youth work, family services, and community safety.

Southwestern Pennsylvania remains our proof of concept and our foundation.

 

Where we are today

Today, Violence Prevention Network USA is building something the country is missing: a civil society response to the early signs of extremist violence. We work in the space between broad prevention and law enforcement, the point where concern is real but the next step is unclear.

What began in Pittsburgh now reaches across the country. Our helpline is national, and our network of practitioners and partners keeps growing.
