Recognize the warning signs. Know what to do.

Most people who turn to violence show signs first. In nearly 8 out of 10 cases, someone close by notices a growing pull toward violence before any harm is done, and doesn't know what to do about it. Our training is for that someone.

 

What you'll take away

We work in the middle space between general awareness and law enforcement, giving people the skills to recognize early warning signs, stay in hard relationships, and act before concern turns into harm. Across our programs, you'll come away better able to:

👁Recognize the early warning signs that someone is moving toward violence

🤝Stay in a hard relationship without carrying it alone

💬Engage someone caught up in hateful or violent ideas in a way that actually helps

🔗Know when and how to bring in VPN for advice or a referral

 

The programs

Community Support Group

For people carrying a hard relationship

Someone you're connected to is being pulled into ideas that justify hostility, hate, or violence — and staying in that relationship is getting harder to manage alone. This group gives you methods for staying connected and recognizing when it's becoming too much to hold by yourself.

Format: 90 minutes, monthly
Fee: Free
