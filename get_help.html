If something feels off, it's okay to reach out.

Whether you're worried about someone you know or about your own thoughts, our helpline is here. It's free, confidential, and you don't have to be sure before you call.

☎ Call 412-756-3643 Write to our team

 

Worried about someone?

Is someone in your life increasingly hostile, justifying hate and maybe violence towards people that are different from them? You don't need to be certain. If something feels wrong, that's reason enough to reach out.

With the right support, people can move away from hate and violence. We'll listen, help you understand what you're seeing, and talk through what you can do next. Reaching out is about getting support, not getting someone in trouble. Every conversation is free and confidential.

Worried about yourself?

If your thoughts are changing in ways that worry you, or you've been drawn to ideas that scare you, you're not alone. People can change while staying true to who they are.

If you're dealing with anger, isolation, or thoughts that concern you, support can help you build a future where you belong. We listen without judgment and help you think through what's going on and what you want next for your life.

 

What you might be noticing

You don't need all of these to reach out. Even one can be worth a conversation.

Pulling away from friends and family

Growing anger or hatred toward a group of people

Talking as if violence is necessary or the only option

Spending more time in extreme online spaces

Believing everyone is against them

Hostility or contempt, with no willingness to talk

 

What happens when you reach out

When you contact us, we can help you:

1

Understand whether what you're seeing is concerning

2

Figure out how to talk with the person

3

Decide what's safe and right for your role

4

Find the right next step, or other support that fits

Some calls end with a plan to start a conversation. Others end with bringing in more help. Either way, we work through it together. You can stay anonymous, and you don't need to give your name or have all the answers.

 

Who you'll talk to

Our helpline is staffed by trained coaches with backgrounds in social work, counseling, mediation, reentry support, and violence prevention. We know how to talk about radicalization, extreme beliefs, and movement toward violence.

We are not a crisis line, and we don't provide therapy or clinical treatment.

Safety note: If someone is in immediate danger, call 911. If you or someone you know is in emotional crisis or thinking about suicide, call or text 988 for the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline.
