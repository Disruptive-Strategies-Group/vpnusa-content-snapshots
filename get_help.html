Worried about someone?

If someone in your life is getting involved in harmful or extreme ideas, you don't have to handle it alone. If something feels off, it's okay to reach out.

With the right support, anyone can move away from harmful beliefs. Our helpline offers free, confidential support. We listen, help you understand what's going on, and talk through what you can do next.

You might have noticed:

  • Pulling away from friends and family
  • Expressing strong anger or hate toward certain groups
  • Saying violence is the only option or needed
  • Spending a lot of time in extreme online content
  • Thinking everyone is against them
  • Refusing to talk but acting hostile
  • Believing in harmful conspiracy theories

Reaching out is about support, not getting someone in trouble.

Worried about yourself?

If you've been drawn to harmful or extreme ideas, or feel like your thoughts are changing in ways that concern you, you're not alone.

People can change while staying true to themselves. If you're struggling with anger, isolation, or harmful beliefs, support can help. Our helpline is free and confidential. We listen without judgment.

Reaching out is a step toward support and change.

Who you'll talk to

Our helpline is staffed by trained coaches with experience in social work, counseling, mediation, reentry support, family support, and violence prevention.

This is not a crisis line and we do not provide clinical treatment. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911. For mental health crisis support, contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988.
